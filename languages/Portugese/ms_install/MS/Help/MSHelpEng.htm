1. About MessageSearch v1.1

"The MessageSearch Program v1.1" in the sermons of W. M. Branham is an all-embracing and convenient to use source of all sermons translated into (...) language. With this program you can search for key words or phrases in the paragraphs of sermons, print out the text of any of the sermons, which is a great help to study the Message of Brother Branham. About options and functions of the program, please read further items of this User's Guide.

2. Updating the program

After you have downloaded or received the update file, save it in the following folder of your computer: ìc:\ms\updatesî. After you have opened the program, click ìAdd sermon from fileî in the ìFileî menu, or press <F5>, and choose the update file(s).

3. Search options and functions

By default the program will make a search through all sermons in the database and the text of the Bible.

There is a possibility to choose sermons you want to run the search in. There are two kinds of the ìtreeî with sermon titles, that appears on the display by clicking icon .

One kind of a ìtreeî (icon ) is a list of sermons by date.

Another (icon ) is a list of titles in the alphabetic order.

Clicking the Bible "tree" (icon ) the list of the Books of the Bible will be displayed.

By marking empty boxes of titles, or years, or Bible Books, you limit the search to these sermons, or years, or the Books of the Bible only.

Double-clicking the title in the ìtreeî will open this sermonís or Bible chapter complete text.

You can hide the ìtreeî by clicking  again, but if you have checked some boxes, the program will still be searching in the marked sermons, or years, or Bible Books. To go to the full search, just switch to a different kind of a ìtreeî.

4. Search words and phrases

It is known by experience, that even using search programs it is still sometimes very hard to find the specific phrase or the word, because a man does not remember the phrase or the word exactly the way it was said, so, please, have patience, before you will be able to find searching words and phrases quick and smooth.

The words for a search are entered in the search line (white line above) after clicking on it. The search is activated by clicking the icon ìsearchî .

You can change the size of the search line, and it will remain like that after you start your program again. To do this, point your mouse cursor between the search line and the button "search". When the cursor changes it's shape, click the left button on your mouse and move it until the desired size.

Contents

4.1. General principles

Since the languages other then English has prefixes and endings that do not sometimes change the key meaning of the word but only serve to match the grammar rules of the language, you have to consider that while entering the words for the search.

The program seeks for any word within a numbered paragraph or a combination of letters within the words in this paragraph, whose are matching your query.

For example, if you want to find all words that has to do with singing, you have to enter the word sing in the search line. Thus the program will find all the words like: sing, singing, singer, sings.

When you enter several words (combinations of letters) for the search, dividing them by a space, the program recognizes them as independent words, whose it will search in any order within the numbered paragraph.

To make the search easier, there are several options, activated by special symbols, that you can use while entering the search words:

whole word (symbol ´.ª [period] );

or (symbol ´=ª);

except (symbol ´‑ª [minus] );

search for a phrase (symbol ´_ª);

which should be entered without spaces after the word.

The symbol brackets ì(ì ì)" designates how many words at once will the symbols listed above will have effect on.

4.2. Capital and small letters

The program searches the words that begin (are written) with capital or small letters by default, the search of words in the search line is not case sensitive.

But sometimes it is important to find words specifically with capital letter. Thus, by pressing the button "case sensitive search" (icon ), words in the search line will be sought for regarding capital and small letters.

Contents

4.3. Whole word

Function whole word (symbol ´.ª or released button ) allows you to find specific form of the word/words in the search line. If the button is released, all words in the search line will be recognized by the program as the only form of those words to search.

If the button is pressed, you can still mark out the word that you want to be found in some specific form, you can do so with symbol ".".

For instance: seven thunders .seal will find all forms of the words Seven Thunders and only this given form of the word seal.

By default, the button  is pressed, so the program will search all forms of the word/words or combinations of letters.

Contents

4.4. Alternative word search function ("or")

Function or (symbol ì=î) is used for an alternative search of two or more different words within the numbered paragraph.

For instance: Holy (Spirit=Ghost) Baptism will find all phrases where there are words Holy Ghost or Holy Spirit.

Contents

4.5. Excluding words to search ("except")

Function except (symbol ì-î) is used to exclude specific word(s) from the search, that you do not want to exist in the same paragraph with the words you want to find.

For instance: -water Holy (Spirit=Ghost) Baptism will find all paragraphs, where it speaks about the Holy Spirit or the Holy Ghost Baptism, but not mentioned the word water.

Contents

5. Search a phrase

Function search for a phrase (symbol ì_î) is used to find specific phrase. The program recognizes these words as one unit.

For instance: thus_saith_the_Lord will find paragraphs where the prophet says THUS SAITH THE LORD.

6. Paragraphs displayed on the screen

In the window ìparagraph countî there is a number, which shows how many paragraphs are being shown in the search window. You can change this value, if you want to see more paragraphs at once in the same window. (By default this value equals 20.) At the end of the line is a number showing the total number of paragraphs found. Between the arrows the numbers of paragraphs currently displayed are shown.

7. Switching between different windows inside the program

With every search, opening the context of the sermon and creating or opening the file of quotes a new window is created, which is present there until you close it or exit the program. Working with the program they can accumulate quite many, so in order to go to the previously opened window of your search, text, context or quote file, click menu title ìwindowsî and choose the window you need. Each type of the window has its own sign:

 - text of the sermon or a chapter of the Bible;

- search results;

- quote file;

- paragraph from the search result in the context of the sermon.

To go to the window with quotes you have selected, click icon ìShow quotes windowî  . The icon will change to . Now if you click it, you will return to the last window you opened.

8. Context of the sermon

Every separate numbered paragraph on the screen can be viewed in the context of the sermon by clicking open the context ; the text of the sermon will appear at the location of the paragraph that you opened the context from.

Contents

9. Print

By clicking on the icon ìprintî , it will print all paragraphs currently displayed in the window (Look ìParagraphs displayed on the screenî.) the way it looks on the screen (highlighted search hits, paragraph headers, notes. Look ìDisplaying search hits and paragraph headersî, and also ìNotesî.)

If you want to print selected quotes only, you will have to move those paragraphs to the Quotes window first. (Look "Moving paragraphs to quote window")

Contents

10. Moving paragraphs to quote window

You can make your own selection of quotes. To do so, put the cursor on the paragraph you want to add to that file, and click the icon ìmove paragraph to Quotes windowî .  It will be moved to a different window, which is a new quote file, that you will be able to name by clicking ìSaveî icon . Later you can add desired quotes to this file, by opening it from the folder under ìOpen quotesî icon . In order to delete a paragraph from the quote file, put the cursor on the quote you want to delete, and click ìDelete paragraph from quote windowî icon .

Contents

11. Notes

The program allows you to make your own notes, which are going to be found under the paragraph header, at the beginning of the numbered file. Putting the cursor on the text (not in the paragraph header) of the paragraph, where you want to add your note, click ìNoteî icon , and type in the text. The notes are not displayed by default, but you can view them, by clicking on the ìDisplay notesî icon .

Contents

12. Displaying search hits and paragraph headers

You can turn off the highlighting of the search hits, by clicking on the ìDisplay search hitsî icon . By clicking on the ìDisplay paragraph headersî icon  you can turn off the paragraph headers above every numbered paragraph (for example, it is convenient if you want to print the whole text of the sermon), but in this case the function ìMoving paragraph to quotes windowî will not work.
